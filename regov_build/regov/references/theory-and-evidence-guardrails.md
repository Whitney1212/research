# Theory And Evidence Guardrails

## Purpose

Use this reference for mechanism reasoning, causal interpretation, literature-backed claims, statistical interpretation, and paper arguments.

## Minimum Claim Structure

For any nontrivial scientific claim, separate:

- observation: what the data show
- processing path: how the result was produced
- interpretation: what the result may mean
- theory warrant: why the interpretation is physically, statistically, or ecologically plausible
- boundary: what has not been corrected, measured, or verified
- counter-check: what would weaken the claim

## Epistemic Status

Label claims when useful:

- `已核验事实`: directly checked in local files or project memory evidence
- `工作假说`: plausible but still under test
- `候选机制`: one mechanism among alternatives
- `敏感性结果`: depends on alternative processing choices
- `不应上升`: evidence is insufficient for a stronger claim

## Anti-Overclaiming Rules

- Do not turn a diagnostic quantity into a final physical flux without checking units, corrections, and method boundary.
- Do not infer causality from temporal co-occurrence alone.
- Do not collapse multiple candidate mechanisms into one clean story.
- Do not use one project's pattern as direct evidence for another project unless the shared conditions are explicit.
- If literature is missing, say the claim needs literature support instead of inventing authority.

## Theory Card Template

Create a theory card when a concept will recur.

```markdown
# Theory Card: <name>

## Claim Boundary
What this concept can and cannot justify.

## Applicable Conditions
Data, scale, assumptions, and corrections required.

## Failure Modes
Common ways this concept is overused or misread.

## Project Links
Relevant workstreams and evidence notes.

## Literature
Full citations or Zotero keys.
```
